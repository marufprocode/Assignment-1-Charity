Millions face challenges to restart lives after worst floods in Bangladesh

Life is gradually returning to normal in northeastern Bangladesh, which was devastated by the worst floods in a century.
However, the suffering of people is far from over as they have lost everything and face new challenges in resuming their lives.

We also supported the deployment of Mobile Medical Teams to provide immediate medical needs and ensure continuity of essential services for those affected.

WHO organized a health cluster meeting on 14 June 2022 to discuss coordinated preparedness and response to the floods this season to reduce death and diseases. Emphasis was on preventing and managing drowning and snake bite that are the main causes of mortality and morbidity during floods in Bangladesh.
